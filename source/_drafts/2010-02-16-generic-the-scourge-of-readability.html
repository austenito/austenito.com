I have a feeling that one day someone is going to update Effective Java with a new item that says:

"Use Generic Judiciously"

First of all you should understand that I'm a dynamic language fan boy.  I love ducktyping.  After all if it looks like a duck and acts like a duck, then by all means it should quack like a duck.  I love languages that are clear and concise and don't require my brain to jump into hyper drive (space balls reference) to have some resemblence of understanding.

So that brings me to Java's generics.

I think it's a useful feature.  A really big hack (look this up), but a useful feature when used correctly.  Hashmap and list are good examples of generics used correctly.
