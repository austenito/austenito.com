--- layout: post title: "You're only as good as those around you" date: 2010-12-25 comments: false ---
The other night while out with some friends, someone brought up the idea that a baseball player in high school wasn't really all that good at his school even though he was a highly touted prospect at another school. the other school's baseball team wasn't all that good so in their eyes, the player was a stud. Not so much at my friends high school.

It's funny how random topics translate into something related in software.

We really are only as good as those surrounding us.

It's nice being the big fish in the small pond.

It's nice when people ask you for help.

But are you really a stud?

But how do you improve? Who do you ask for help? Are you really good at what you do?

I've been thinking about this a lot lately. I played baseball for Damien. We aren't really all that good and I was a starter. A decent enough player. But would I start on the big boy schools? Definitely not. Was my growth stunted due to my surrounding team? Probably.

how do I fix this?

how can I seriously improve where I am and where I'm going? maybe it's time to figure out a 5 year goal. Maybe I can work towards playing for the big boys.

I probably need to set up some sort of plan.
