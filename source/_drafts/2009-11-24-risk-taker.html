I have been spending a a lot of time rebalancing my financial portfolio over the past couple months.  I've been thinking about what is my level of risk?

I came across and interesting article yesterday called 40-30-30 Rule: Why Risk Is Worth It.  This article talks how learning new skills is 40% physical, 30% skills related, and 30% of taking risks.

It's kind of interesting how taking risks are a part of life.  People say that you should be taking risks in your financial portfolio and in life experiences when you are young because you have less to lose.  You don't have a home, spouse, kids, resposibilities.  Once you get any number of those things your tolerance for risk goes way down.

What about taking risks in your career?  Learning new things in the computer science field is a must.  I try take risks by trying to learn new languages (currently Ruby and Rails), reading a bunch of technical blogs and books, and focusing on improving my technique at work.  The 40-30-40 article talks about playing a game by always using the same strategy.  You don't really grow when you keep using the same strategies or you might not be able to find a better strategy.  This past year I've been focusing on trying to keep an open mind and listening to what other developers have to say.  This works out really well because I get to see other developers perspectives on the problem.  After all, people are smart, and they often see things in a different light.

I was reading taking financial risks (link to how much risk you should take) and started thinking about other types of risk.  What types of risks do I take in my life?

when I think about it, I really haven't thought about my goals smartly. I have a couple of goals, become an awesome coder, get to the technical lead level, release something.

My financial life is on track im keeping stats on how im doing each month and where i need to go.

Why dont i do this with my career? For one thing it's really hard to measure my current goals. And since it's hard to measure, it's hard to achieve them in a timely manner.
